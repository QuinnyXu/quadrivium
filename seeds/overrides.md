# Override Log

When the Maintainer overrides an agent verdict, log it here with one paragraph: what was proposed, what was overridden, the reasoning, and the kill condition that would prove the override wrong. Periodic panel reviews check overrides against outcomes — that is where the calibration happens, not in-session.

Format: date, agent overridden, proposed bet, verdict, override, kill condition.

  - *Agent overridden:* Vision (the v1.0.0-fedsupport deposit-DOI-shape step-1 consultation).
  - *Proposed bet (Vision):* shape **(c)** — deposit fedsupport as a standalone **manual** Zenodo record with its own pre-reserved concept DOI, honoring Decision B (separate concept DOI per dataset) and §10 (one repo) by bending the deposit *mechanism* to manual upload.   - *Override (Maintainer):* reframe one level up, at **product identity** — the citable unit is the **integrated harmonized database, NOT the individual survey**. ONE repo → ONE concept DOI (`10.5281/zenodo.20404785`) for the whole database; datasets are subtrees; each GitHub release mints a **version DOI** under the one concept via the standard Zenodo integration. This **OVERTURNS Decision B** (it is an overturn, not an amendment). It also dissolves the problems (c) was solving: no manual deposit, no pre-reservation inversion, no automation loss, and the cross-survey spine gets an unambiguous home (the database).
  - *Reasoning:* (1) **thesis-is-relatedness** — the product IS the integrated whole; the cross-survey relatedness (the join spine especially) is the value, and per-dataset DOIs fragment exactly that relatedness and *orphan* the cross-survey spine (no per-survey deposit can own a relationship-level artifact); (2) the **verified Zenodo fact-find** (2026-05-30) — per-dataset DOIs would have forced **permanent manual deposit** (the GitHub integration nests new releases as versions under the repo's own concept DOI and cannot auto-create a second standalone concept for the same repo), a standing operational tax the reversal eliminates. Vision's (c) was well-reasoned *below* the reframe; the override is at product-identity, not a correction of Vision's logic at its own level.
  - *Kill condition (what would prove the overturn wrong):* if a future dataset needs **independent citation, independent licensing, or an independent release cadence** the single-concept model cannot express — e.g. a survey citable/versioned on its own clock decoupled from the database, or a survey whose license/embargo differs from the database's CC-BY-4.0 such that bundling under one DOI misrepresents its terms — then the one-concept model is too coarse and per-dataset (or a hybrid: database concept + satellite concepts for divergent-license surveys) must be revisited. While every survey is CC-BY-4.0, NCSES-sourced, and released on the database cadence, the overturn holds.

- **2026-05-29 — Forward dollar-floor mis-calibration (HD 3.2 spine).** The forward kill condition on the FedSupport identity spine set a **~80% dollar-match floor** (scope-doc §11 forward kill, PRIMARY) as an abstract coverage target. The floor *fired correctly* — the MVP came in at 73.1% dollar-match, the surface-to-Vision reflex triggered, and the surface revealed the genuine structural finding (HERD's era-B `ipeds_unitid` is canonical-not-complete; the gap is an IPEDS-cycle deliverable, not a converging manual tail). So the mechanism worked. But the *number* was mis-set: ~80% was chosen as a generic "good coverage" target, not derived from the join target's actual keyspace completeness. The spine's true ceiling is **~80.7% dollars** (the best-case manual ceiling, and only if every ambiguous flagship is hand-resolved to the correct campus UNITID), driven by HERD's own era-B UNITID coverage (94.3% of rows, 4.2% of distinct institutions NULL at source). A forward floor of ~80% against a join target whose UNITID-coverage ceiling is ~80.7% leaves a ~0.7-point window — the floor was effectively un-clearable by in-scope effort, which is *why* it fired, but the firing read as "the spine underperformed" when the accurate read is "the floor was set above the join target's structural ceiling." **Calibration:** forward coverage floors for cross-survey spines must be **derived from the join-target's actual keyspace completeness** (here, HERD's era-B UNITID coverage), not from a generic coverage target. This is the **third calibration finding this sprint** and the second of the mis-set-metric family — it mirrors the §11 kill-metric correction (which pinned the *axis*: dollar not institution, and the *pass*: post-mechanical not raw-first-pass); this one pins the *level*: the floor must be anchored to the target keyspace's ceiling, not asserted abstractly. The reflex is kept (an un-clearable floor that surfaces a real structural finding is the asymmetry working as designed); only the level-setting discipline is corrected. **Kill condition for the captured guidance:** if a future cross-survey spine's join-target keyspace completeness is genuinely unknowable at floor-setting time (no way to measure the target's key coverage before building the spine), then a generic floor is the only available instrument and this guidance does not apply — revisit whether "derive from target completeness" is always feasible or only when the target is already harmonized in-repo. No outcome harm; the MVP ships at its characterized ceiling per principal ratification.

- **2026-05-30 — Grain-tier figure corrected by generator-honesty (HD 3.2 receipt, Option A).** The HD 3.2 identity-spine receipt's grain tier (system-vs-campus flagship ambiguity, Seam B) was ratified at **8.2% / 16 institutions / 81.3% best-case ceiling** — a figure that cleared Vision's verdict, panel review, AND principal ratification. It was wrong. The number came from a throwaway recovery spike (since superseded by the build's own gate-evidence pass) counting candidate *name-variant rows* (`len(cands)`): an institution with multiple HERD name spellings that all resolve to ONE UNITID (e.g. IUPUI, 3 spellings → a single UNITID) was double-listed as grain-ambiguous. When the receipt generator was rebuilt under Option A (generator-as-source-of-truth, §3 reproducibility — the generator must emit the deposit artifact so a rebuild reproduces it), it was forced to derive the decomposition from the actual spine and counted *distinct UNITIDs* — the correct grain measure — yielding **7.6% / 10 / 80.7%**. The other two tiers reproduced exactly; the IUPUI distinct-UNITID resolution independently cross-validates the gate check-(a) 0.6%-recoverable finding (two counts agree). **Calibration / meta-lesson:** build-derived truth beat consensus-ratified assumption — a figure can clear verdict + review + ratification and still be wrong if it rests on a hand-computed counting choice no one re-derived from source. The defense that caught it was *structural, not vigilance*: making the generator the single source of truth surfaced the error **by construction** on the next rebuild, where three rounds of human review had not. This is the **fourth calibration finding this sprint** and the strongest evidence for the generator-as-source-of-truth discipline — a hand-curated receipt would have shipped 8.2%/81.3% silently into the deposit. SHIP-(A) holds a fortiori on the corrected figure (Vision re-affirmed 2026-05-30; the correction moves the same direction as the conclusion — gap marginally more structural, ceiling now only ~0.7pt above the floor). **Kill condition for the captured guidance:** if generator-emission of a narrative receipt ever ossifies framing that genuinely needs editorial revision across rebuilds (the generator becoming a barrier to legitimate prose improvement), revisit the all-generated posture toward a generator/hand-authored split for the editorial layer — but the HD 3.2 receipt's narration was fully data-derivable (zero un-templatable sentences surfaced), so the all-generated form was correct here. No outcome harm; the correct figure ships.

The sentence both inverted the comparison AND drew the dangerous-direction conclusion (the exact misread the clause-(c) receipt exists to prevent), and it **self-contradicted the very next sentence**, which correctly named the SAFE direction. It survived generator-emission, framing-authorship, the structural-summary review, AND the figure/structure ratification; it was caught only when the principal read the **verbatim** narration paragraph against the numbers, pre-push. Fixed in the generator's `_write_receipts` template (Option A — not a hand-edit), rebuilt byte-stable (the v1.0.0 gate artifact ships correct from the start). **Calibration / meta-lesson (the sharpened one):** the cold-reader-facing **prose is itself a summary-maskable artifact**. The figures were right and the structure was right; a structural summary confirmed both and nearly closed the gate — but the *sentence about* the numbers was false. Prose is a **separate correctness surface** from the figures it describes, and it passed emission + framing-authorship + summary-review on a sentence that contradicted its own data. This is the latest numbers-vs-narration mismatch the read-the-actual-artifact discipline has surfaced this sprint (after the §11 kill-metric axis/pass correction, the floor mis-calibration, and the grain-counting quirk) — the fifth calibration finding overall, and the fourth caught by reading the artifact rather than its summary. **Every future deposit has a narration layer, and every narration layer is summary-maskable.** The locked discipline: **structural summaries confirm structure; only verbatim reads confirm the words are true** — the narration paragraph of any deposit-facing artifact must be read as text, against the numbers, before the artifact gates a release. **Kill condition for the captured guidance:** if verbatim narration reads add no catches beyond structural review across the next several deposit artifacts, relax to spot-reads — but four read-the-artifact catches in one sprint argues the other way. No outcome harm; caught pre-push.

- **2026-05-30 — Byte-stability discipline must cover EVERY generated deposit artifact, not just the one under suspicion (HD 3.2 spine, 6th calibration finding).** A §3 reproducibility defect was caught pre-push: the FedSupport identity spine CSV (`crosswalks/_shared/institution_identity.csv`) was **non-deterministic** — two consecutive `build_fedsupport_identity_spine.py` runs produced different SHA-256 (F5FBB29… vs 1427277…). The cause: when ≥2 HERD era-B display names map to one matched UNITID, the `herd_inst_name_long` alias was picked by unordered DuckDB `SELECT DISTINCT` iteration (`uid_to_name.setdefault`), so the manufactured display name flapped run-to-run (~123 of 1,506 rows on those two runs; 203 UNITIDs structurally at risk). UNITID, match_method, rates (55.8%/73.1%), and row order were stable — **only the human-readable alias flapped**, and a committed deposit artifact that must rebuild bit-equivalently per §3 had shipped into the commit. Fixed with a documented tiebreak (HERD era-B row-frequency then alphabetical, stated in the affected rows' `decision_rationale` — a manufactured display value ships with its rule, like every other manufactured value in this project); rebuilt byte-stable (df74f9bb… across three consecutive runs). **The meta-lesson — and why this is a calibration finding, not just a bug fix:** the receipt (`identity_spine_match_rate.md`) got the byte-stability (Option-A two-build) check *because we were actively editing it*; the spine CSV — **equally generator-emitted, equally a deposit artifact** — did not get the check because it was "done" and was never re-run-and-compared. Non-determinism sits dormant in a finished artifact precisely because finished artifacts stop being rebuilt. **The generalized discipline: before any deposit ships, ENUMERATE every generator-emitted artifact in the deposit set and run the two-build SHA gate on EACH — not only the one under active edit.** A clean sweep was run on the full FedSupport generated set (spine CSV: stable after fix; spine receipt: stable; obligations parquet: byte- AND data-stable, not even invoking the §3 writer carve-out); `type_of_activity_map.csv` and `fedsupport_parse_reconciliation.md` are hand-authored and out of the gate by definition. This is the **sixth calibration finding this sprint** and the first in the reproducibility-determinism family (the prior five were metric mis-calibration ×2 and numbers-vs-narration ×3). The fix is also promoted into `docs/release_runbook.md` as a hard named pre-deposit gate (the finding has forward leverage — it must fire on GSS/SED/IPEDS too, not only FedSupport). **Kill condition for the captured guidance:** if a future generated artifact is provably deterministic by construction (e.g., a pure passthrough of an already-hashed input with no manufactured/aggregated values and no unordered iteration), the two-build SHA is redundant for it — but the cost of running it is ~one rebuild, so the gate stays mandatory-by-default and the exemption must be argued per artifact, not assumed. No outcome harm; caught pre-push, v1.0.0 held.
