### 2026-04-29

**HERD's contribution is taxonomy resolution, not panel construction.** Raw HERD is already long-format keyed by `(institution, year, questionnaire_no, question, row, column)`. Most of "harmonization" is filtering and crosswalking codesets, not pivoting. The methods note's framing shifts from "we built a panel" to "we resolved the taxonomies that make the panel readable across 50 years." Sharper claim, more defensible. The breakpoint timeline becomes the natural lead chart. *Tags: HERD methods note, framing, lead chart.*

**Standard-form vs. all-respondents reconciliation.** NSF Table 26 publishes the standard-form aggregate ($1M+ FY23 R&D); HERD microdata includes both standard- and short-form respondents. Reconciling the harmonized panel against published totals requires filtering to the published population, not summing all rows. External scholars miss this because they read the data table, not the methodology. Worth a footnote in the methods note, possibly its own paragraph. *Tags: HERD methods note, footnote-becomes-paragraph, reconciliation.*

### 2026-04-30

**HERD encoding contamination 2014–2017 — promoted (2026-04-30 after HD 1.5).** Cross-year audit completed via HD 1.5's per-year profile. Pattern: 2010 has 1 Latin-1 substitution (the lone Veteran's-apostrophe), 2011–2013 are clean, 2014–2017 cluster carries 149 / 158 / 159 / **308** invalid-UTF-8 byteranges per year (monotonic growth), 2018+ is clean again. The shape strongly suggests an upstream NSF data-export pipeline change — Windows-1252 free-text fields started flowing through unconverted in 2014, then NSF normalized to UTF-8 export starting 2018. Confirmed one-chart explainer candidate. The data is loud enough to support a standalone methodology-extension piece: encoding contamination 2014–2017 as the kind of artifact only people who load the bytes ever see. **Per-byte log full population deferred to HD 2.x as a deliberate audit run** (HD 1.5 used the no-write scan path; `validation/reports/encoding_substitutions.csv` currently has only the 2010 verification entry). *Tags: HERD methods note, possible methodology-extension piece, Reconstructive Harmonization axis (encoding-pipeline drift).*

**The cross-question summation rule (era A → era B field totals).** HD 1.4's de-risking finding: 2010 fragmented era-A's single `question = 'Expenditures by S&E field'` into multiple era-B source-class questions, so 50-year longitudinal field-level analysis requires reconstructing era-A field totals as `Federal field-level + Nonfederal field-level` in era B. Most external scholars treating HERD as a longitudinal panel either (a) don't notice and silently break their time series at 2010, or (b) restart the panel at 2010 and lose 38 years of pre-redesign coverage. The summation rule + its institution-year-level validation is a standalone methods-note section. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension piece, Reconstructive Harmonization clause (a).*

**Question-count trajectory chart as alternate lead chart.** 7 → 19 questions in one year at the 2010 cliff, settled at 16–19 through 2024. Simpler than the per-discipline ratio chart, harder to misread, needs no summation rule to construct. Possible companion to the four-bar decomposition: question-count chart as problem visualization with decomposition chart as contribution visualization, or vice versa, or both as paired figures. *Tags: lead chart, methods note, possible companion figure.*

**Encoding chart as standalone artifact candidate.** The 2014–2017 contamination seed is the cleanest candidate for a standalone methodology-extension piece distinct from the 2010 era-boundary methods note. The data is loud enough to defend a one-chart explainer in its own right ("For four years NSF was shipping mojibake to researchers who had to handle the bytes" — operationally striking, methodologically defensible). Decision deferred until the 2010 era-boundary methods note ships and reception is measured. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension piece, Reconstructive Harmonization axis (encoding-pipeline drift).*

### 2026-05-01

**Discontinuities the program inherits.** The methods note's §7 (Reconstructive Harmonization, the methodological signature applied) closing committed the framing to three additional discontinuities beyond the 2010 HERD break: era-A pre-1981 field-list variability, era-B FY 2017 micro-expansions, and the personnel sibling deposit's Q15/Q16 schema change at FY 2020. Each is potentially its own short methodology-extension piece — smaller in scope than the 2010 break, sharing the same methodological signature, distinct in audience and surprise. The pre-1981 piece argues against scholars who casually use HERD back to 1972 without checking the field-list change. The FY 2017 piece names four undocumented era-B-only fine-leaf additions (industrial-and-manufacturing engineering, natural-resources-and-conservation, materials science, anthropology) that affect any analysis of those fields' apparent emergence. The Q15/Q16 piece pairs naturally with the personnel sibling. Three potential pieces; all defer until the 2010 piece ships and reception is measured. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension pieces, Reconstructive Harmonization application.*

**Training-grants boundary as the cross-source seam.** The methods note's §5 explicitly bounded what HERD alone cannot decompose: the split between real R&D-spending growth and era-B definitional expansion at fixed-cohort grain, with training grants as the largest unmeasurable component. Per FY 2024 Guide pages 80–83 and 630–633, training grants are bundled into Q9 + Q11 source rollups in era B with no separate carve-out question. Closing that part of the residual requires NIH RePORTER (training-grant award totals at institution-year grain) or NSF Award Search (training-grant award activity codes) joined to HERD institution-year. This anchors a future cross-source linkage piece to a specific bounded gap the 2010-boundary methods note already named — the piece's contribution becomes "we closed the training-grants component of the unmeasurable residual the 2010 boundary methods note bounded." The reader contract is already in place: methods note readers know what's missing and why; the linkage piece delivers it. *Tags: methods note continuation, bounded gap, cross-source linkage candidate.*

### 2026-05-02

**FY-2023-back-applied standard-form filter pattern (NCSES Data Table 26).** NCSES defines the "standard form" population for HERD personnel reporting as institutions reporting ≥$1M in FY 2023 R&D expenditures, then back-applies that membership filter to all earlier years' published Table 26 totals. The result: FY 2022 and FY 2023 verification totals diverge from a free-sum of all FY-year respondents by ~0.46–0.88% — small enough to look like a build error on first read, but structurally explained once the back-application is understood. The pattern is visible in three places: the published Table 26 totals (post-filter), the public-use microdata (pre-filter, all respondents), and the A-3 response-rate table (which itself reports two universe denominators). This is a footnote in the personnel sibling methods note today; it is plausibly a paragraph in a later piece on NCSES universe definitions across HERD reporting tables, and possibly a standalone methodology-extension piece on how operational data infrastructure builders should treat back-applied membership filters as Reconstructive Harmonization edge cases — verifiable but not free-summable. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension piece, NCSES universe definitions, footnote-becomes-paragraph, Reconstructive Harmonization application.*

### 2026-05-10

**Publication-regime stability as a Reconstructive Harmonization axis (umbrella seed).** Operational data publication regimes drift independently of the underlying data; verification-anchor selection has to account for regime stability, not just data availability. NSF reduced FY 2024 HERD DST from 86 to 55 PDFs and pushed Tables 28–54 to Table-Builder-only at HD 2.4.a — the data scope was unchanged, the static-anchor architecture wasn't. The deposit's reproducibility contract had to face the regime change explicitly. This is a fourth axis of operational-data discontinuity, alongside three already documented: the back-applied universe filter pattern (FY-2023-anchored standard-form filter on HERD personnel, 2026-05-02), the encoding-pipeline drift pattern (HERD 2014–2017 mojibake contamination, 2026-04-30), and the taxonomy-redesign pattern (the 2010 era boundary that the methods note's Reconstructive Harmonization framing already names). Three is a pattern; this is the fourth axis. The methodological signature extends to verification-anchor selection — clause (a) ("reconstruct what each era can support on its own terms") applies to the publication regime an anchor is staged against, not just to the underlying microdata. Promoted into the HD 2.4 scoping doc §2(e) and the methods-note reproducibility paragraph at `docs/methods_notes/reconstructive_harmonization.md` §6 (locked 2026-05-10). Possible methodology-extension piece on its own surface — the regime-change framing that "the static PDF you cited last year may not exist this year, and a deposit-grade anchor architecture has to plan for it" is operationally striking and methodologically defensible. Inherits to future dataset migrations when publication-regime drift is encountered. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension piece, Reconstructive Harmonization application, umbrella seed.*

**Early-1970s codeset retirement — fifth axis instance.** HD 2.4.b round 1 surfaced FY 1973–1974 raw HERD files emitting a Guide-undocumented `status='c'` code on field-level `column='Total'` rows (13 affected cells; full characterization in `validation/reports/era_a_status_codeset_findings.md`). The same files emit `status` codes in mixed case (`'I'` / `'E'` alongside `'i'` / `'e'`); FY 1990–2009 carry no `'c'` and no uppercase. This is a fifth instance of the publication-regime stability axis above, distinct from the four already named because it sits at the **status-code grain rather than the question-structure grain** — discontinuities live at multiple grains (question structure, file-level encoding, publication-regime architecture, status-codeset), and the inventory's coverage broadens to match. Framing: *early-1970s codeset retirement* — NSF used a status code (`'c'`, plus upper-case variants) in FY 1973–1974 that disappears from raw files by FY 1975 and is not in the FY 2024 Guide's documented codeset. Whether `'c'` represents a 1970s vintage code (NCSES Academic R&D Expenditures Survey methodology) NSF later abandoned, or a corruption that survived the migration to HERD's reformulation, is open — an NCSES historical-publications documentation hunt remains a pending diagnostic task; if a documented semantic surfaces, the framing language adjusts (from "abandoned codeset" to "documented codeset NSF stopped exercising"). Five named instances of operational-data publication-regime discontinuity, each at a different grain, is the inventory threshold where the regime-change framing has the body to support its own piece. *Tags: methods note, possible methodology-extension piece (graduation candidate), Reconstructive Harmonization application, codeset-extension policy precedent.*

### 2026-05-21

**UCSF Engineering structural absence — sixth axis instance.** HD 2.4.g entry sub-action's FY 2024 Table Builder verification grid surfaced a substrate-shape finding parallel to the publication-regime drift: the FY 2024 export staged 2026-05-21 (`data/reference/dst-table-builder/dst-table-builder-FY2024.csv`, SHA-256 `e0fc1f7b…6738a6`) contains 58 substantive cells, not the nominal 60 (10 institutions × 3 disciplines × 2 source classes). UCSF (inst_id 001319) is missing both Engineering cells (federal and nonfederal). Diagnosis: UCSF is a health-sciences-only institution; the UC system Engineering R&D base lives at Berkeley, San Diego, Los Angeles, Irvine, and Davis — not San Francisco. The cell absence is structural at the institution-substrate level (no Engineering school exists), not suppression or NCSES disclosure-rule artifact. This is the sixth instance of the publication-regime / operational-data discontinuity axis, sitting at the **cohort-substrate-heterogeneity grain** — parallel to FY 1973–1974 status='c' codeset retirement (fifth instance, status-codeset grain), but at the institution-substrate level rather than the encoding-codeset level. The 10-institution cohort does not imply 10-institution coverage of any given discipline; the verification grid's cell count is not derivable from cohort × discipline × source-class multiplication alone and requires institution-level substrate verification. Framing: *cohort substrate composition speaks through the cell-count* — a deposit-grade verification grid that contracts honestly to what the substrate supports is the right disposition (Path A locked 2026-05-21); coercing the grid back to nominal with imputed structural-absence zeros papers over the methodological distinction between "documented zero in an existing discipline" and "no discipline exists at this institution." Six named instances at six distinct grains continues to compound the inventory's body weight for the eventual graduation question. *Tags: methods note, Reconstructive Harmonization application, possible methodology-extension piece (graduation candidate), substrate-shape axis, cohort-heterogeneity grain.*
