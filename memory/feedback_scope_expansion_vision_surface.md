Under quadrivium's 7-step agent-team working pattern, when Skipper would **expand scope beyond a locked or literal instruction** — e.g., de-staling surrounding prose during a placeholder swap, reconciling adjacent stale text, correcting a runbook's file/hash list — prefer to **surface the proposed expansion to Vision (or the principal, if principal-territory) BEFORE applying it**, when feasible. Do not only surface it transparently at completion.

**Why:** the principal noted at Stage 3 Phase C (2026-05-26) that Skipper's DOI-swap de-staling (changing "reserved / minted-at-release" framing that was false post-mint, beyond the literal `XXXXXXX → DOI` string swap) was *methodologically correct and transparently surfaced* — but under strict new-pattern adherence it would have routed to Vision before application. Transparent-surfacing-at-completion closes the gap after the fact (principal disposes; Vision assesses at step 5); surfacing before application closes it before the edit lands, which is cleaner and keeps Vision's assessment role intact. The same principal has repeatedly rewarded surface-before-act discipline across HD 2.4 and the Stage 1/2/3 arc.

**How to apply:** when a literal instruction would otherwise produce an incoherent or false artifact (so an expansion is genuinely warranted), and surfacing is feasible (not a time-critical inline necessity), surface the proposed expansion to Vision first — rather than applying it and flagging at completion. Transparent-flag-at-completion remains the **floor** (never silent-resolve a scope expansion); Vision-surface-before-application is the **preferred ceiling**. Mid-execution discoveries route to Vision (the new pattern's step 4/5 channel) unless the call is purely principal-territory (personal identity, scope-beyond-the-approved-plan, CLAUDE.md edits).
